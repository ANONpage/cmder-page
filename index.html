We presents CMDer, a novel mode-decomposition-based diffusion framework that enables high-quality motion generation and editing from a single motion sample, with interpretable frequency-domain control and applications in cross-modality synthesis and crowd animation.

Abstract

Realistic motion generation remains challenging due to the scarcity of large-scale, high-quality datasets, particularly for animals and custom characters. It necessitates effective learning from limited data to produce diverse and controllable synthetic motion.

We present CMDer (pronounced Commander), a Controllable Mode Decomposition-based Single Motion Synthesis framework that leverages diffusion models to generate diverse motions from single instances. CMDer performs mode-based motion decomposition to represent a motion sequence using intrinsic mode functions, which offers a frequency-oriented and interpretable approach to motion analysis and synthesis.

The learning framework of CMDer is efficient and lightweight, built upon a shallow U-Net diffusion model that learns to generate various motions. We also design a Mode Enhancement Block (MEB) to improve local motion modelling.

Our results show that CMDer outperforms existing methods in both controllability and motion quality. We demonstrate various applications in motion generation and editing, such as mode-based synthesis, cross-modality motion synthesis, and controllable crowd animations.

Architecture

CMDer is a novel pipeline that generates diverse and editable character animations â€” all from a single motion example. The CMDer architecture consists of three components: (1) the Motion Mode Decomposition Module (MMDecomp) for intrinsic mode decomposition, (2) the Motion Mode Diffusion Network (MMDiffusion), a shallow U-Net improved with Mode Enhancement Block(MEB), and (3) the Motion Mode Composition Module (MMComp), which fuses the diffusion outputs to synthesize final motions.
